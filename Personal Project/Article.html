SO MANY POLLS, WHICH ONE TO TRUST?

A poll is an attempt to represent the publics' opinion regarding a specific topic. In this case, our polls are representative of the political opinion that the citizens of the United States hold, for what it pertains the upcoming 2020 election.

In a world where fake-news is on the constant rise, and the spread of mis-information is at reach of only a few keyboard strokes. It is imperative for the reader to understand and know, not only the results of the polls, but as well who is behind those same polls.

This is where the chart below comes into play. Starting from the left, we have the names of the pollsters (these are the companies that have done the polling). Next, in the middle we have the category (based on grade) to which each individual pollster belongs to. Finally, to the right we have the number of polls associated with each pollster in its respective category.

At first it might not be that intuitive to read this chart, but please bear with me for, it does indeed provide some insights. Pollsters are given grades A through D. At first one might think that these grades for what it pertains pollster's reputation might be dependent on the factors such as the people behind and the amount of years that they 've been in business. Yet, this finding might indeed be true, but the chart to the right allows us to find a further dependence. In general,the pollsters with the highest grades, are the ones that do the greatest number of polling. You heard that right, someone who has done, say 30, different polls is more likely to have a higher grade reputation than someone who has only done 10 or 15.

Insights: This visualization invites the viewer to pay attention to the reputation of the pollster, but also puts them on the lookout, for a high reputation grade does not necessarily point to the pollster's reputation, but it could be due to the high number of polls that they have put forth.

Hence, when looking at a poll with an A rating vs a poll with a B rating do not discard the results of polls with a B grade simply based on the grade reputation, given such discrepancy could be based on the number of polls.

"*There are indeed exceptions to this general finding."

SO, WHAT ARE AMERICANS SAYING...

IN GENREAL?

The first and foremost information that is presented are the overall results; that is what will American's vote in terms of party. It is worth noting that in the Unites State of America there are a total of 3 different parties to choose from: Democratic, Republican, or Liberal.

Insights: At the moment the U.S. vote is split between "Democrats (46%)" and "Republicans (44%)" . Although, the majority of the votes go to either the Republican or Democratic party, the "Liberal (5%)" party does gain a meaningful 5% of the U.S forecasted vote.

This is quite a tight race with very small margins of difference. It is important for both parties to truly take this race seriously given that, at least for the moment being, no obvious winner can be claimed just yet.

BY REGION?

We were able to see in the previous visualization that race for the presidential candidacy is going to be quite a close race, and as a result both parties must make appropriate decision and choices that will allow them to win the hearts of the indecisive ones. What are these choices, and what do they consist of? Well, at first is important to have an overall idea of what each specific region of the United States is expected to vote.

In the below visualization, we represent the percentage of the different votes for party according to the 4 distinct regions of the United States. Such regions being West, Midwest, South, and Northeast. For some polls we have there was no region annotated, we have still left these results in the visualization under the option "no location."

Insights: from this visualization we are able to note that Democrats are most likely to win in the West, the Republicans are most likely to win in the South; Finally, the true battle for the presidency will be "fought" in states belonging to the Midwest and the Northeast region of the United Sates.

BY INDIVIDUAL STATE?

Until now we have been able to see the general forecasted results for the presidential race, furthermore we were able to divide the U.S. in different regions and were able to pick up on different trends among different regions. Now, we dive deeper into the individuals states given winning these ones is what will allow each candidate party to get closer to presidency. In this section, we dissect the states that haven't made their mind yet, and are likely to go with either party. We call these states "swing states. Finding out the "swing state" allows each respective party to plan their rally's and public events in places were their words will matter and have an effect. For it doesn't make sense (politically speaking) to go in a state where the majority of the population has made their mind up. Hence, finding "swing states" is like finding "water in the desert"- very precious.
